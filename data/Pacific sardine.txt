the scientific name of pacific sardine is sardinops sagax. the south american pilchard, sardinops sagax, is a sardine of the family clupeidae, the only member of the genus sardinops. it is found in the indo-pacific oceans. its length is up to 40 cm. it has a number of other common names, some of which more appropriately refer to subspecies, including blue pilchard, australian pilchard, blue-bait, californian pilchard, chilean sardine, japanese pilchard, pacific sardine, and southern african pilchard.
