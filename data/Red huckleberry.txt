the scientific name of red huckleberry is vaccinium parvifolium. red huckleberry (vaccinium parvifolium) is a species of vaccinium native to the western north america, where it is common in forests from southeastern alaska and british columbia south through western washington and oregon to central california. in the oregon coast range, it is the most common vaccinium. it occurs mostly at low to middle elevations in soil enriched by decaying wood and on rotten logs, from sea level up to 1,820-metre . the fruit is an edible red to orange berry 6-millimetre (0.24 in) to 10-millimetre (0.39 in) in diameter.
