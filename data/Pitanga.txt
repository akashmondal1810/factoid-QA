the scientific name of pitanga is eugenia uniflora. the pitanga, surinam cherry, brazilian cherry, or cayenne cherry (eugenia uniflora) is a plant in the family myrtaceae, native to tropical south america's east coast ranging from suriname to southern brazil, as well as parts of paraguay and uruguay. known as pitanga throughout brazil or íïangapirí_ in surrounding countries, the plant is relatively pest resistant, easy to grow and high in antioxidants. the surinam cherry is often used in gardens as a hedge or screen. the tree was introduced to bermuda for ornamental purposes but is now out of control and listed as an invasive species.flowers develop into ribbed fruits 2 to 4 cm in diameter, starting out as green, then ranging through orange, scarlet and maroon as they ripen.
