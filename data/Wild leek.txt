the scientific name of wild leek is allium ampeloprasum. allium ampeloprasum is a member of the onion genus allium. the wild plant is commonly known as wild leek or broadleaf wild leek. its native range is southern europe to western asia, but it is cultivated in many other places and has become naturalized in many countries.allium ampeloprasum has been differentiated into three cultivated vegetables, namely leek, elephant garlic and kurrat. in tidewater virginia, the plant is commonly known as the "yorktown onion."wild populations produce bulbs up to 3 cm across. scapes are round in cross-section, each up to 180 cm tall, bearing an umbel of as many as 500 flowers. flowers are urn-shaped, up to 6 mm across; tepals white, pink or red; anthers yellow or purple; pollen yellow.
