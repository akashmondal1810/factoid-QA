the scientific name of celery stalks is apium graveolens var. dulce. celery (apium graveolens var. dulce) is a plant variety in the family apiaceae, commonly used as a vegetable. the plant grows to 1 m (3.3 ft) tall. the leaves are pinnate to bipinnate with rhombic leaflets 3?6 cm long and 2?4 cm broad. the flowers are creamy-white, 2?3 mm in diameter, and are produced in dense compound umbels. the seeds are broad ovoid to globose, 1.5?2 mm long and wide.celery is used around the world as a vegetable for the crisp petiole (leaf stalk). the leaves are strongly flavoured and are used less often, either as a flavouring in soups and stews or as a dried herb.
