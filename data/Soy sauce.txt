soy sauce (also called soya sauce) is a condiment produced by fermenting soybeans with aspergillus oryzae or aspergillus sojae molds, along with water and salt. after the fermentation, which yields fermented soybean paste, the paste is pressed, and two substances are obtained: a liquid, which is the soy sauce, and a cake of (wheat and) soy residue, the latter being usually reused as animal feed. soy sauce is a traditional ingredient in east and southeast asian cuisines, where it is used in cooking and as a condiment. all varieties of soy sauce are salty, earthy, brownish liquids intended to season food while cooking or at the table.
