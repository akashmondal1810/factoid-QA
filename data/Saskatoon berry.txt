the scientific name of saskatoon berry is amelanchier alnifolia. <i>amelanchier alnifolia</i>, the saskatoon, pacific serviceberry, western serviceberry, alder-leaf shadbush, dwarf shadbush, chuckley pear, or western juneberry, is a shrub with edible berry-like fruit, native to north america from alaska across most of western canada and in the western and north-central united states. historically, it was also called pigeon berry. it grows from sea level in the north of the range, up to 2,600 m (8,530 ft) elevation in california and 3,400 m (11,200 ft) in the rocky mountains, and is a common shrub in the forest understory.
