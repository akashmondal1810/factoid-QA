the scientific name of horseradish tree is moringa oleifera. moringa oleifera is the most widely cultivated species of the genus moringa, which is the only genus in the family moringaceae. english common names include: moringa, drumstick tree (from the appearance of the long, slender, triangular seed-pods), horseradish tree (from the taste of the roots, which resembles horseradish), ben oil tree or benzoil tree (from the oil which is derived from the seeds). it is a fast-growing, drought-resistant tree, native to the southern foothills of the himalayas in northwestern india, and widely cultivated in tropical and sub-tropical areas where its young seed pods and leaves are used as a vegetable.
