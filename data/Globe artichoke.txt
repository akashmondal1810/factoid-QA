the scientific name of globe artichoke is cynara scolymus. the globe artichoke (cynara cardunculus var. scolymus) is a cultivated variety of a species of thistle. it is cultivated as a food. the edible matter is buds that form within the flower heads before the flowers come into bloom. the buds go away or change to a coarse, barely edible form when the flower blooms. the uncultivated or wild variety of the species is called a cardoon. it is a perennial plant native to the mediterranean region.
