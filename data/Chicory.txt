the scientific name of chicory is cichorium intybus. common chicory, cichorium intybus, is a somewhat woody, perennial herbaceous plant usually with bright blue flowers, rarely white or pink. many varieties are cultivated for salad leaves, chicons (blanched buds), or for roots (var. sativum), which are baked, ground, and used as a coffee substitute and additive. it is also grown as a forage crop for livestock. it lives as a wild plant on roadsides in its native europe, and in north america and australia, where it has become widely naturalized.
