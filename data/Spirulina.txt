the scientific name of spirulina is spirulina. <i>spirulina</i> represents a biomass of cyanobacteria (blue-green algae) that can be consumed by humans and other animals. there are two species, <i>arthrospira platensis</i> and <i>arthrospira maxima</i>.<i>arthrospira</i> is cultivated worldwide; used as a dietary supplement as well as a whole food; and is also available in tablet, flake and powder form. it is also used as a feed supplement in the aquaculture, aquarium and poultry industries.
