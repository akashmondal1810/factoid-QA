the scientific name of broad whitefish is coregonus nasus. the broad whitefish (coregonus nasus) is a freshwater whitefish species. dark silvery in colour, and like a herring in its shape, its distinctive features include a convex head, short gill rakers, and a mild overbite. it is found in the arctic-draining streams, lakes, and rivers of far eastern russia and north america. its prey includes larval insects, snails, and shellfish. it is eaten by humans and brown bears.
