the scientific name of black cabbage is brassica oleracea var. viridis. the acephala group refers to any type of <i>brassica</i> which grows without the central 'head' typical of many varieties of cabbage. these are included within the species <i>brassica oleracea</i>, such as kale (<i>brassica oleracea</i> var. acephala). the name literally means "without a head" in contrast to those varieties known as <i>capitata</i> or "with a head". this group includes a number of species, both wild and cultivated, many of which are grown for their edible leafs and flowers.
