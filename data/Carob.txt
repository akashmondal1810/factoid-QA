the scientific name of carob is ceratonia siliqua. ceratonia siliqua, commonly known as the carob tree and st john's-bread, is a species of flowering evergreen shrub or tree in the pea family, fabaceae. it is widely cultivated for its edible pods, and as an ornamental tree in gardens. the ripe, dried pod is often ground to carob powder which is used as a substitute for cocoa powder.
