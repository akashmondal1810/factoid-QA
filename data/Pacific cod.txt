the scientific name of pacific cod is gadus macrocephalus. the pacific cod, gadus macrocephalus, is an important commercial food species. it is also known as gray cod or grey cod, and grayfish or greyfish. it has three separate dorsal fins, and the catfish-like whiskers on its lower jaw. in appearance, it is similar to the atlantic cod. a bottom dweller, it is found mainly along the continental shelf and upper slopes with a range around the rim of the north pacific ocean, from the yellow sea to the bering strait, along the aleutian islands, and south to about los angeles, down to the depths of 900 meters (~ 3000 feet). may grow up to 1 m (39") and weigh up to 15 kg (33 lbs). it is found in huge schools.
