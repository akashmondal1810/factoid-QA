the scientific name of snapper is lutjanidae. snappers are a family of perciform fish, lutjanidae, mainly marine, but with some members inhabiting estuaries, feeding in fresh water. some are important food fish. one of the best known is the red snapper.snappers inhabit tropical and subtropical regions of all oceans. they can grow to about 1 m (3.3 ft) in length. most feed on crustaceans or other fish, though a few are plankton-feeders. they can be kept in aquaria, but mostly grow too fast to be popular aquarium fish. they live at depths reaching 450 m (1,480 ft).
