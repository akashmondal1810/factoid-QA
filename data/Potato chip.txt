potato chips (known as crisps in british and hiberno english; either chips or wafers in indian english; and chips in american, australian, new zealand, canadian, singapore, south african, and jamaican english) are thin slices of potato that are deep fried. potato chips are commonly served as an appetizer, side dish, or snack. the basic chips are cooked and salted; additional varieties are manufactured using various flavorings and ingredients including seasonings, herbs, spices, cheeses, and artificial additives.
