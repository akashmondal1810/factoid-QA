the scientific name of blue crab is callinectes sapidus. callinectes sapidus (from the greek calli- = "beautiful", nectes = "swimmer", and latin sapidus = "savory"), the blue crab, atlantic blue crab, or regionally as the chesapeake blue crab, is a species of crab native to the waters of the western atlantic ocean and the gulf of mexico, and introduced internationally.c. sapidus is of significant culinary and economic importance in the united states, particularly in louisiana and in the chesapeake bay. it is the maryland state crustacean and is the state's largest commercial fishery.
