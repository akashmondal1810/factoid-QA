the scientific name of rosemary is rosmarinus officinalis. rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a woody, perennial herb with fragrant, evergreen, needle-like leaves and white, pink, purple, or blue flowers, native to the mediterranean region. it is a member of the mint family lamiaceae, which includes many other herbs. the name "rosemary" derives from the latin for "dew" (ros) and "sea" (marinus), or "dew of the sea". the plant is also sometimes called anthos, from the ancient greek word, meaning "flower".rosemary is used as a decorative plant in gardens and has many culinary and medical uses. the plant is said to improve the memory. the leaves are used to flavor various foods, such as stuffings and roast meats.
